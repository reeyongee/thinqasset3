# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Investment managers, private equity and venture capital sponsors, financial institutions, and family offices evaluating regulated fund infrastructure. They arrive mid-diligence: comparing hosting vs greenfield licensing, testing whether ThinqAsset feels institutional enough for LPs and regulators, and scanning for a clear path to launch.

## Product Purpose

ThinqAsset’s public marketing site explains the regulated fund platform, structures, and corporate infrastructure so sponsors can understand the operating model, choose a path, and book a consultation. Success is confidence: the site should feel as precise as the platform it sells.

## Brand Personality

Institutional · precise · international — with enough craft and motion to feel bespoke, not brochure-ware. Professional first; quietly playful in rhythm, reveal, and composition.

## Anti-references

- Generic fintech purple gradients and glassmorphism kits
- Dense PDF-brochure pages pasted onto the web
- Inter / Roboto / “enterprise SaaS” card grids
- Cartoon icon rows and pill-badge clutter
- Dark-mode crypto aesthetic for regulated finance

## Design Principles

1. **Infrastructure as craft** — The regulated stack is the product; show operating clarity, not buzzwords.
2. **One composition per viewport** — Hero and chapter opens read as deliberate scenes, not dashboards.
3. **Motion with manners** — Scroll and entrance choreography earn attention; never decorate every element.
4. **Trust through specificity** — Named jurisdictions, concrete capabilities, and honest scope beat vague “world-class” claims.
5. **Template that doesn’t feel templated** — Shared offering architecture, per-offering content and visual accent so sixteen pages feel like a series, not clones.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA as the baseline. Prefer reduced-motion fallbacks for all GSAP sequences. Maintain readable contrast on light institutional surfaces; never rely on color alone for hierarchy.
